## Part B - Survey Evaluation:

Identify and describe survey features:

```
1. Sample type: Stratified simple random sampling, given the ten provinces were divided into 27 geographic strata (14 major CMAs plus grouped smaller CMAs and non-CMA areas), with simple random samples drawn without replacement within each stratum. The survey also used "rejective sampling"â€”terminating interviews early for a portion of non-volunteers (0â€“50% by province) to allocate more effort toward finding the harder-to-reach volunteer population.

2. Sample size: Target was 20,000 respondents; actual was 16,149 (excluding rejected cases). Total households in scope: 47,436.

3. Target population: Persons aged 15 and older in Canada's ten provinces, excluding residents of the territories (Yukon, NWT, Nunavut) and full-time residents of institutions.

4. Sampling frame: Constructed from two sources: (1) lists of landline and cellular telephone numbers available to Statistics Canada, and (2) the Address Register of all dwellings in the ten provinces. About 86% of phone numbers were linked to addresses; the rest were included unlinked. Sampling units were groupings of phone numbers tied to the same address. Households without telephones were excluded from the frame but accounted for in weighting.

5. Survey mode(s): Two modes were offeredâ€”self-completed online questionnaire (rEQ) and computer-assisted telephone interview (iEQ). This was the first GSS cycle to offer online completion. Households with matched addresses received mailed invitations with a secure access code. About 52% of responses came via the online mode. Proxy interviews were not permitted.

6. Timeline: Data collection ran from September 4 to December 28, 2018. Most questions referenced the 12 months preceding the interview.

7. Response rate: 41.9% overall. Of 47,436 in-scope households, 49.9% were household-level non-response, 8.2% person-level non-response, 34.0% completed responses kept, and 7.8% were rejected under the rejective sampling protocol.

8. Weights: The person-level weight (WGHT_PER) adjusts for probability of selection, three-stage non-response, rejective sampling, and calibration to external population totals (stratum counts, 2017 Canadian Income Survey income distribution, and province-age-sex distributions). For variance estimation, 500 bootstrap replicate weights are provided.

9. Data processing: Responses were captured electronically in both modes. Write-in responses were coded using NAICS 2017 (industry), NOC 2016 (occupation), and ICNPO (non-profit organizations). Flow editing followed a top-down strategy, and derived variables were created. For the public-use file, donation values were perturbed and rounded to protect confidentiality.

10. Cleaning, imputation, etc.: Missing or off-path responses were coded as "Valid Skip" or "Not Stated." Donor imputation was used for item and partial non-response, matching recipients to similar donors via a score function. Imputation was done in nine steps covering income (about 18% imputed via tax linkage), volunteering hours, and donation variables (16.6% of donation totals came from imputed values).

11. Sources of error: Sampling error is addressed through bootstrap variance estimation, with CV thresholds guiding release (under 16.5% acceptable, 16.5â€“33.3% flagged, over 33.3% suppressed). Non-sampling errors include interviewer/respondent mistakes, data capture errors, non-response bias (mitigated by weight adjustments), and coverage error from excluding phoneless households.

12. Limitations, known biases, etc.: The 2018 cycle introduced online collection, making direct comparisons with prior iterations inappropriate per Statistics Canada. Other concerns include potential over/under-coverage from frame construction, mode effects between online and phone responses, and recall bias given the 12-month reference period.

13. Link to codebook (also in folder 02_activities/assignments/gss_codebook/2018_GSS33GVP_PUMFGuide.pdf): https://abacus.library.ubc.ca/api/access/datafile/95995
```
